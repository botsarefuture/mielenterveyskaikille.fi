Mental Health for Everyone - Demonstration on March 9, 2024

The Mental Health for Everyone demonstration is a peaceful and substance-free family event and expression, demanding investments and actions to solve the mental health crisis.

For Whom?

We welcome all entities that cherish care, peace, non-violence, and human rights to join together in advocating for mental health. Welcome all ordinary individuals, families, organizations, associations, networks, professionals, and entrepreneurs. And, of course, those suffering from mental health problems!

The Mental Health for Everyone demonstration is a way for citizens to declare that the cycle of distress must be broken. We demand from the government substantial investments in well-being and political actions to address mental health problems.

Where?

The Mental Health for Everyone demonstration takes place in Helsinki. We march from Senate Square to the Parliament House. Local satellite demonstrations are encouraged to be organized simultaneously throughout Finland.

When?

Sun, March 9, 2024

Why?

At least every fifth Finn experiences mental health disorders within a year, and every fifth gets depressed during their lifetime. Nowadays, over half of all disability pensions are granted due to mental health and behavioral disorders. The increasing prevalence of mental health problems reflects a change in society. To change the direction of development, it is essential to address, among other things, politics, working conditions, and social structures that increase people's mental burden.

However, in the 21st century, mental health services have not been invested in to the same extent as other healthcare. The share of mental health services in healthcare expenditure has been declining throughout the 21st century, and in 2021, it was only 4.5 percent of all expenditures. Psychiatric care gets an increasingly smaller share of specialized healthcare funding. [1]

WHO has stated that good mental health also means good physical health. Mental health and public health, human rights, and socioeconomic development are inseparably linked. Investing in mental health is an investment in a better life and future for all of us. [2]

The demonstration calls for the government to make substantial investments in well-being and concrete political actions to address mental health problems.
