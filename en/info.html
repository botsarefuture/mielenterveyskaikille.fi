Mental Health Belongs to Everyone! - Protest on June 3, 2024

The Mental Health Belongs to Everyone! - Protest is a peaceful and substance-free event for the whole family, with the purpose of demanding investments and concrete actions to solve the mental health crisis.

For whom?

Everyone that values care, peace, non-violence, and human rights are more than welcome to come together to protest for mental health. All ordinary people, families, organizations, associations, networks, professionals, and entrepreneurs are welcome. And of course, those with mental health issues!

The Mental Health Belongs to Everyone! - Protest is a way for citizens to express that the cycle of distress must be broken immediately! We demand significant investments in well-being from the government and political action to address mental health issues.

Where?

The Mental Health Belongs to Everyone! - Protest will take place in Helsinki. We will march from the Senate Square to the location of the after-event (to be announced later). Local satellite protests are encouraged to be organized simultaneously across Finland.

When?

Monday, June 3, 2024

Why?

At least one in five Finns has to experience mental health disorders annually. One in five Finns has to go through depression during their lifetime. Currently over half of all disability pensions are granted due to mental health and behavioral disorders. The increasing prevalence of mental health problems reflects a change in our society. If we want to change the direction of this development, it is important to address factors such as politics, working conditions, and societal structures that increase people's mental burden.

However, investments in mental health services have not been in correlation with the amount of patients. The share of mental health services in total healthcare expenditure has been declining throughout the 21st century and was only 4.5 percent of all expenditure in 2021. Psychiatric care receives an increasingly smaller share of funding for specialized healthcare. [1]

The WHO has stated that good mental health also contributes to good physical health. Mental health and public health, human rights, and socioeconomic development are inseparable. Investing in mental health is an investment in a better life and future for all of us. [2]

The protest demands significant investments in well-being from the government and concrete political actions to address mental health issues.
