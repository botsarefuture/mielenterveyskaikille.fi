Demands

1. Preventive Measures

What?

There must be a strong emphasis on initiatives that promote mental health and prevent mental health problems.

Why?

By investing in prevention and early intervention, we can prevent the backlog of mental health services and reduce distress.

2. Raising awareness

What?

We need to raise public awareness of the importance of mental health and provide information on ways to improve mental wellbeing.

Why?

Awareness of mental health problems and open dialogue lay the foundations for a stigma-free society that supports mental health.

What?

4. Inclusion of communities

What?

The sense of belonging to a community and inclusion in society, which strengthens individuals' social networks and supports mental health must be promoted.

Why?

Communities create a safety net for individuals to relay onto. Being part of a community also reduces loneliness. Loneliness has been shown to have a negative impact on mental health.

5. Accessible mental health services

What?

General accessibility to mental health services must be guaranteed to everyone.

Why?

Accessible services aim to achieve equality of access to care. Accessibility of services also contributes to time-efficient access to care.

6. Mainstreaming mental health into policy

What?

Mental health must be taken into account when making decicions in politics.

Why?

Political decisions have a direct impact on people's mental well-being, for example in terms of the resources budgeted for mental health services. We need policies that support mental health, enable well-being and maintain functional capacity.

7. Effective and long-term mental health solutions

What?

A commitment to provide long-term and comprehensive mental health services.

Why?

Short-term therapy is an ineffective response to complex mental health problems. Mental health problems need long-term support that meets individual needs and promotes full recovery.
